Introduction

Welcome to my website! My name is Andrew Clare-Green, I am a 3rd year student majoring in Computer Science at the University of Guelph. This website will highlight my experience working for the University of Guelph in the Registrar Office as a Co-op student. This includes what I accomplished during this work term, as well as what I learned and reflection on my goals I created throughout my term.

About the Employer

Located in Guelph, Ontario, Canada, the University of Guelph is home to nearly 30,000 students, offering 94 undergraduate degrees, 48 graduate programs, and 6 associate degrees. The university was originally established on May 8th, 1964, from merging Ontario Agricultural College (OAC), the Ontario Veterinary College, and the Macdonald Institute. Later that year, Wellington College was also added to provide opportunities in arts and sciences.

I worked in the Office of Registrarial Services which is made up of a variety of departments which recruits and admits students, provides financial support, schedules courses and exams, and produces transcripts and diplomas. I worked in the technological department where I was tasked with a variety of technical jobs which allowed me to gain hands on learning experiences and further develop my abilities.

Goals

I created a variety of goals at the beginning of my work term that I aimed to achieve before completing my internship with the university.

As a computer science major, I am always looking to improve and learn about technology. As technology is constantly changing, one of my goals was to improve my technological literacy by learning more about computer hardware and software and expanding my skill set. More specifically, I hoped to learn and use a new programming language or one that I didn't have as much experience with, to work on real world projects in the office. I was able to do so with Visual Basic and SQL. I was tasked with reviewing and updating code which challenged my debugging skills and ability to understand code that was previously written. This can be benefital for future work experience as I gained hands on experience working with Visual Basic and SQL and continued to develop my problem solving skills and ability to critically think.

Job Description

In this position, I had a variety of tasks ranging from hardware projects to software development to supporting technical documentation. This includes updating and maintaining a variety of technical documents for the university. During my work term I also edited and debugged Visual Basic and SQL code, or XML code for forms found on the university website. I also gained experience in excel, processing a variety of files which included applicants, transfer students and their information. I was responsible for setting up user accounts, managing security measures, and troubleshooting access issues. This role developed my communication skills within a work environment, my technical literacy, and my ability to meet deadlines.

Conclusions

Overall, this position was an excellent work experience as it provided me with a solid understanding of a variety of technology. I was able to gain hands on experience working full time hours both individually and along side my boss.
