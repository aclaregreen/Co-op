Introduction

Welcome to my work term report! My name is Andrew Clare-Green, and I am currently a 3rd year student majoring in Computer Science at the University of Guelph. This report will highlight my experience working as a Co-op student in the Regisrar's Office at the University of Guelph in the Systems and Technologies department. Throughout this report I will discuss the accomplishments I have achieved during this work term, the skills I developed, and reflect on the goals I set for myself at the start of the term.

About the Employer

The University of Guelph, located in Guelph, Ontario, Canada, is home to nearly 30,000 students, offering 94 undergraduate degrees, 48 graduate programs, and 6 associate degrees. The university was established on May 8th, 1964, following the merger of the Ontario Agricultural College (OAC), the Ontario Veterinary College, and the Macdonald Institute. Later that year, Wellington College was added, expanding the university's opportunities in arts and sciences.

During my work term, I was employed in the Office of Registrarial Services, which contains a variety of departments responsible for student recruitment and admissions, financial support, course and exam scheduling, and the production of transcripts and diplomas. I worked within the technological department, where I undertook various technical tasks, allowing me to gain hands-on experience and further develop my technical skills.

Goals

At the beginning of my work term, I set several goals to achieve by the end of my internship with the university. As a computer science major, I am always eager to enhance my knowledge and skills in technology. Given the rapidly evolving nature of technology, one of my primary goals was to improve my technological literacy by deepening my understanding of both computer software and hardware. Additionally, I aimed to expand my skill set by learning and applying a new programming language or one with which I had limited experience.

During my work term, I successfully achieved this goal by working with Visual Basic and SQL. I was tasked with reviewing and updating code, which required me to use my debugging skills and my ability to comprehend and work with existing code. This experience was valuable for my future career, as it provided me with hands on experience in Visual Basic and SQL and further developed my problem solving abilities and critical thinking skills.

Another goal that I created for myself to acheieve throughout this work term was to improve my written communication skills. Strong written communication is essential in ensuring that instructions and information are clearly understood, particularly in technical fields. Throughout my work term, I was responsible for creating various types of technical documentation, including user guides and internal process instructions. It was essential that these documents were not only clear but also precise, as they were used many different users. The effectiveness of these documents directly impacted their ability to complete tasks accruately. By focusing on structure, clarity, and detail, I ensured that the content I created could be easily understood and followed by others which improved overall workflow and user experience. This experience significantly contributed to my growth as a student and strengthened my ability to translate complex technical concepts into digestable information for a non technical audience.

Job Description

In this role, I was responsible for a variety of tasks, including software development, hardware projects, and supporting technical documentation. I contributed to updating and maintaining several technical documents for the university and gained practical experience in editing and debugging Visual Basic, SQL, and XML code for forms on the University's website. Additionally, I processed Excel files containing information about applicants, transfer students and their academic data, uploading them in a test environment to check for errors before uploading in the production database.

My responsibilities also included setting up user accounts, managing security protocols, and troubleshooting access issues. This role not only improved my technical literacy but also enhanced my communication skills within a professional environment and strengthened my ability to meet tight deadlines.

Conclusions

Overall, this position provided me with an exceptional work experience and a solid understanding of various technologies. I had the opportunity to gain hands on experience by working full-time hours both independently and collaboratively with my supervisor.
